The Long Way Round

The Journey was Everything but not linear. Yet every twist added a lesson, every setback a story, making it unforgettable.

Five exams, one drop year, and a path shaped by both triumphs and setbacks—each bringing me closer to where I am today.

01
The First Ride
Where it all Began...
Got my life's first crush when I was in Standard 7th. She turned my whole life and motivated me to be good at studies and become a good person in life. Next, my friend introduced to this thing called "Olympiads" which became the foundation of my science curiosity. And due to this crush and curiosity, I ended up scoring 93% in 10th Boards.
02
The Pilot
"Dai Dai, Ikou, Dale, Allez, Lez Goo!"
The Olympiads were just the stepping stone. It was now time to move one step further. Took PCM after my 10th Boards, and took the hard path—started preparing for JEE.
03
The Setback
Lost as a Student, Lost as Son, Lost as a Friend, and most importantly, Lost as a Lover...
As the JEE approached closer, there were many emotions driving me crazy! Proposed to my crush, got rejected. Locked in for the exams, not be able to crack even the Mains in my first attempt. Result? Depression...
04
The Drop Year
Even You, deserve a Second Chance!
The setback that reset the approach. Strong board results followed, but the real shift was learning to prepare properly, not just harder. Took a long rest before the drop year and got super locked in for everything that mattered to me — For Her, For Me, For God & For My Family
05
The Comeback
Absolute Cinema!
Five competitive examinations, cleared. Proof that the second attempt, prepared properly, beats the first attempt every time. I had every reason to smile. So did my family. But the one message I kept hoping for never came...
06
The Present
IISER Thiruvananthapuram — Year Two
BS-MS Dual Degree, majoring across Physics, Data Science and Mathematics, working as an undergraduate researcher alongside coursework.
07 (Thala for a Reason)
The Future
And if you believe in God, Hope & Believe for a Good Future too...
The current target: First Thesis dedicating to Her. Then, Postgraduate Physics Education overseas, leading into research that refuses to stay inside one discipline.

Where This Is Headed

Four roadmaps. One direction.

Some Long-term plans...

Academic The Scientific Roadmap

IISER first, a PhD abroad next, interdisciplinary research after that. The plan is deliberately not narrow.

  • Finish the BS-MS Dual Degree across Physics and Data Science
  • Build a research profile spanning theoretical physics and computation
  • Target postgraduate physics programmes abroad
  • Land in research that sits between disciplines, not inside one
Technology The Techno-Guy Roadmap

A parallel track aimed at the technology side of the same curiosity — Android, AI, CPUs, GPUs, Computer Vision, and what comes after the current interface paradigm.

  • Deepen applied AI and computer vision skills
  • Build toward future human-computer interaction systems
  • Stay close to Android and applied ML ecosystems
Vision The Multidisciplinary Scientist Blueprint

The Iron Man and Oppenheimer-inspired long game: not one specialism, but real fluency across several.

  • Physics as the core discipline
  • AI as applied extensions
  • Writing as a future area of depth
  • Science communication running through all of it
Admissions Future Planning

The practical track underneath the ambition — figuring out Postgraduate Physics study without pretending funding isn't part of the decision.

  • Research scholarship-friendly postgraduate physics programmes
  • Prioritise affordability alongside academic fit
  • Build the application profile early, not last-minute
